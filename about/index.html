Background

Catrina Turner is the Principal and CEO of Imminent Flair LLC, a cybersecurity consulting firm operating at the intersection of enterprise security, quantum readiness, and strategic risk management. Her work spans Zero Trust architecture, post-quantum cryptography migration, AI-driven threat modeling, and the governance frameworks boards need to manage cybersecurity as a fiduciary obligation.

"The gap between what executives are told about cybersecurity and what they actually need to know to govern it is the most dangerous vulnerability in most organizations."

Zero Hour Intelligence is the public advisory platform of Imminent Flair. It publishes when the threat landscape demands it — not on a content calendar. Each piece is written for decision-makers: precise, actionable, and free of the noise that characterizes most security writing aimed at practitioners rather than leaders.

Focus Areas

Catrina's advisory practice concentrates on the domains where technical risk meets strategic decision-making: the transition from classical to post-quantum cryptography and what it means for organizations whose data has a long sensitivity horizon; the governance and liability implications of NIS2, SEC cybersecurity disclosure rules, and the EU Cyber Resilience Act; the emerging threat surface created by AI-native adversarial capabilities; and the board-level communication frameworks that allow cybersecurity risk to be governed with the same rigor applied to financial and operational risk.

In addition to consulting engagements, Catrina pursues board seat opportunities and quantitative research positioning, with particular focus on post-quantum security economics and AI-driven threat modeling methodologies.
